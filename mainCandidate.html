Important Notes

  • To make testing more efficient and fair, we've listened to feedback from Infra and taken a lot of that feedback into consideration behind the updated competition rules. Please see our new competition rules, which cover more scenarios with detailed explanations.
  • There will be a 5-minute penalty time for each wrong submission.
  • To ensure the fairness of the competition, Aware Technology Solutions for Bussiness hides some test cases during the competition. When users submit invalid documents, Aware Technology Solutions for Bussiness will not show the hidden test cases to users.
  • The final score of this competition will be updated within 5 business days after the competition.

The following actions are considered competition violations:

  • A user may submit an entry using multiple accounts during the contest. An LCUS account and an LCCN account are considered separate accounts, even if they belong to the same user.
  • Multiple accounts submit similar codes for the same issue.
  • Creating unwanted distractions that interfere with other users' participation in the competition.
  • Disclosing the contest method in a public discussion thread before the end of the contest.

ATS Code Testing takes the fairness and justice of our contests very seriously. We absolutely do not tolerate any behavior that violates the rules (e.g. plagiarism, cheating, etc.). When a user is found to be violating the contest rules, we will apply the following penalties to this user:

  • First violation: Match score resets to zero, LeetCoin amount resets to zero, and match and chat are banned for 1 month.
  • Second violation: Match points will be reset to zero and account will be permanently suspended without appeal.

We also encourage all participants to support our efforts in maintaining the fairness and justice of our competitions. Users who submit valid abuse reports will receive additional point:

  • For each violating participant, the first 10 users to report the violation to that participant will receive 20 point each.
  • Each user can earn up to 100 point for reporting in-match abuse.
  • Users will not receive point rewards for reporting on LCCN users.
